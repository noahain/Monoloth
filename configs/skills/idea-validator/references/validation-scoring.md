# Validation Scoring Rubric

## Scoring Dimensions

Six dimensions, each scored 1-5, weighted by their predictive importance for idea viability.

| Dimension             | Weight | Rationale                                                                                                                                                     |
| --------------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Problem-Solution Fit  | 25%    | The most common cause of startup failure is building something nobody wants. This is the most predictive single dimension.                                    |
| Market Opportunity    | 20%    | A strong solution in a non-existent or declining market fails. Market size and timing gate all other dimensions.                                              |
| Financial Viability   | 20%    | Unit economics determine whether the business can sustain itself. Equal weight to market because a large market with broken economics still fails.            |
| Competitive Position  | 15%    | Important but secondary — many successful companies entered crowded markets. What matters is defensibility and differentiation, not absence of competitors.   |
| Technical Feasibility | 10%    | Most ideas are technically buildable. This dimension matters primarily when novel technology is required or when technical complexity creates execution risk. |
| Timing & Environment  | 10%    | Macro factors can enable or kill an otherwise sound idea. Lower weight because timing is hard to assess precisely and can shift.                              |

## Detailed Scoring Criteria

### Problem-Solution Fit (25%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | Problem is urgent, frequent, and quantifiably costly. Customers actively seek solutions and pay for workarounds. Solution directly addresses the core job better than all alternatives. Strong evidence of demand (waitlists, pre-orders, organic search volume). |
| 4     | Problem is real and acknowledged by target customers. Existing solutions leave meaningful gaps. Solution addresses the primary job with a clear advantage on at least one dimension. Some demand evidence exists.                                                 |
| 3     | Problem exists but is not top-of-mind for customers. Existing solutions are adequate but not great. Solution offers improvement but the switching calculus is uncertain. Limited demand evidence.                                                                 |
| 2     | Problem is assumed rather than validated. Customers may not recognize or prioritize it. Solution is a marginal improvement over status quo. No demand evidence beyond the founder's conviction.                                                                   |
| 1     | Problem is fabricated or only affects the founder. No evidence customers experience this pain. Solution is looking for a problem. The job-to-be-done is unclear or nonexistent.                                                                                   |

### Market Opportunity (20%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | TAM exceeds $10B and growing at 15%+ annually. SAM is well-defined and reachable. SOM supports a $100M+ business within 5 years. Market timing is favorable — infrastructure exists, demand is accelerating, regulatory tailwinds. |
| 4     | TAM is $1-10B with steady growth. SAM is identifiable and the team can plausibly capture meaningful share. Timing is good — market is established but not saturated.                                                               |
| 3     | TAM is $100M-1B or growth is flat. SAM requires significant market education or behavior change. Timing is neutral — neither particularly favorable nor unfavorable.                                                               |
| 2     | TAM is under $100M or declining. SAM is unclear or requires the product to create a new category. Timing is questionable — too early (infrastructure not ready) or too late (market consolidating).                                |
| 1     | Market is negligible, declining, or non-existent. No credible path to meaningful revenue. Timing is wrong — critical enablers are missing or the window has closed.                                                                |

### Competitive Position (15%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | Clear differentiation with sustainable moats (network effects, proprietary data, regulatory advantage). No direct competitor addresses the same job for the same segment. Porter's Five Forces analysis shows favorable industry structure. |
| 4     | Meaningful differentiation from existing competitors. At least one defensible advantage that compounds over time. Moderate competitive intensity with room for a new entrant.                                                               |
| 3     | Differentiation exists but is not strongly defensible. Competitors could replicate the advantage within 12-18 months. Industry structure is moderately competitive.                                                                         |
| 2     | Weak differentiation. Strong incumbents serve the same job. No clear moat beyond execution speed. High competitive intensity with well-funded players.                                                                                      |
| 1     | No meaningful differentiation. Competing against entrenched players with superior resources, distribution, and brand. Commoditized market with race-to-bottom pricing.                                                                      |

### Financial Viability (20%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | LTV:CAC ratio exceeds 5:1. Gross margins above 70%. Payback period under 6 months. Clear path to profitability at modest scale. Unit economics improve with scale. Revenue model is proven in the market. |
| 4     | LTV:CAC ratio of 3-5:1. Gross margins 50-70%. Payback period under 12 months. Profitability achievable within 2-3 years at projected growth. Unit economics are sound.                                    |
| 3     | LTV:CAC ratio of 2-3:1. Gross margins 30-50%. Payback period 12-18 months. Path to profitability depends on assumptions that have not been tested. Some unit economics concerns.                          |
| 2     | LTV:CAC ratio below 2:1. Gross margins under 30%. Payback period exceeds 18 months. Profitability requires significant scale that is not yet justified. Unit economics are unfavorable.                   |
| 1     | Unit economics are fundamentally broken. CAC exceeds LTV. Negative gross margins. Revenue model is unproven and speculative. No credible path to financial sustainability.                                |

### Technical Feasibility (10%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ----- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | Solution is buildable with proven, mature technology. No novel technical risk. Small team (2-4 engineers) can ship MVP in 4-8 weeks. Infrastructure is commodity. Build vs buy decisions are clear. |
| 4     | Solution requires integration of known technologies in a somewhat novel combination. Technical risks are identified and mitigable. MVP achievable in 2-3 months with a competent team.              |
| 3     | Solution requires some technically challenging components. One or two significant technical risks. MVP timeline is 3-6 months. Requires specialized expertise.                                      |
| 2     | Solution requires novel technical approaches or immature technology. Multiple significant technical risks. MVP timeline exceeds 6 months. Requires rare expertise or expensive infrastructure.      |
| 1     | Solution requires fundamental technical breakthroughs. Depends on technology that does not yet exist in production-ready form. Timeline is unpredictable. Technical feasibility is unproven.        |

### Timing & Environment (10%)

| Score | Criteria                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5     | Multiple macro trends converge to enable this idea now. Regulatory environment is favorable or creating new opportunities. Technology infrastructure recently matured. Cultural or behavioral shifts create demand. Investment climate is supportive. |
| 4     | Timing is generally favorable. Key enablers are in place. No significant macro headwinds. Market conditions support new entrants.                                                                                                                     |
| 3     | Timing is neutral. No strong tailwinds but no headwinds either. Macro environment neither helps nor hinders. The idea could work now but is not especially timely.                                                                                    |
| 2     | Some macro headwinds. Regulatory uncertainty, economic downturn affecting the target market, or technology infrastructure gaps. Timing is early or late by 1-2 years.                                                                                 |
| 1     | Severe macro headwinds. Regulatory prohibition, technology not ready, economic conditions hostile to the model, or cultural resistance. Timing is fundamentally wrong.                                                                                |

## Computing the Overall Score

Weighted score calculation:

```
overall = (PSF × 0.25) + (MO × 0.20) + (CP × 0.15) + (FV × 0.20) + (TF × 0.10) + (TE × 0.10)
```

Where:

- PSF = Problem-Solution Fit score (1-5)
- MO = Market Opportunity score (1-5)
- CP = Competitive Position score (1-5)
- FV = Financial Viability score (1-5)
- TF = Technical Feasibility score (1-5)
- TE = Timing & Environment score (1-5)

## Verdict Thresholds

| Score Range | Verdict       | Meaning                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 4.0 - 5.0   | **Strong**    | Pursue aggressively. The idea addresses a real problem, in a sizable market, with defensible differentiation, sound economics, and favorable timing. Key risks are manageable.           |
| 3.0 - 3.9   | **Promising** | Proceed with targeted validation. The idea has merit but key assumptions are unproven. Design experiments to test the riskiest assumptions before significant investment.                |
| 2.0 - 2.9   | **Risky**     | Significant concerns must be addressed. One or more dimensions have fundamental weaknesses. Do not invest further until the weakest dimensions are strengthened or the idea is reshaped. |
| 1.0 - 1.9   | **Weak**      | Fundamental issues present. The core hypothesis is flawed or the conditions do not support it. Consider pivoting to a different problem, customer, or solution, or abandon.              |

## Red Flag Overrides

Certain findings override the numerical score regardless of how well other dimensions perform:

| Red Flag                                                  | Override             |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Legal impossibility                                       | Cap verdict at Weak  |
| Ethical violation                                         | Cap verdict at Weak  |
| Physical impossibility                                    | Cap verdict at Weak  |
| Founder-only problem (no external demand)                 | Cap verdict at Risky |
| Negative unit economics with no credible path to positive | Cap verdict at Risky |
| Regulatory prohibition in target market                   | Cap verdict at Risky |
| Requires technology that does not exist                   | Cap verdict at Risky |
| Market is actively shrinking at >10% annually             | Cap verdict at Risky |

Red flags must be explicitly stated in the report with their override effect.

## Confidence Assessment

For each dimension, assess the confidence level of the score:

| Confidence | Meaning                                                                                                                             |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **High**   | Score is based on verifiable data, published research, or direct evidence. Changing inputs would not significantly alter the score. |
| **Medium** | Score is based on reasonable inferences from available data, industry benchmarks, and analogies. Some key assumptions are untested. |
| **Low**    | Score is based on limited data, founder claims, or speculation. The score could shift significantly with new information.           |

Report the average confidence across dimensions. If average confidence is Low, explicitly state that the overall verdict should be treated as preliminary and that targeted research is needed before acting on the assessment.

## Score Calibration Examples

### Problem-Solution Fit: Score 3 vs Score 4

**Score 3 example:** A tool that helps recruiters write better job descriptions. The problem exists (bad job descriptions reduce applicant quality) but recruiters do not rank it as a top pain point. Existing templates and AI writing tools partially address it. The improvement is real but incremental.

**Score 4 example:** A tool that automates SOC 2 compliance evidence collection for SaaS startups. The problem is acute (SOC 2 is required to sell to enterprises, current process takes 3-6 months of engineer time). Existing solutions are expensive ($50K+) or require dedicated compliance staff. Clear demand signal from startup forums and communities.

### Market Opportunity: Score 3 vs Score 4

**Score 3 example:** Niche B2B vertical SaaS for independent veterinary clinics. TAM is limited (~$500M), growth is steady but not explosive (5-7% annually). Market is fragmented with many small players.

**Score 4 example:** Developer tools for AI model deployment. TAM is $5B+ and growing at 25% annually. Infrastructure spend is accelerating. Clear buyer (engineering teams) with budget authority.

### Financial Viability: Score 3 vs Score 4

**Score 3 example:** Consumer subscription app at $9.99/month. CAC estimated at $30 via paid social. Average retention is 4 months (LTV ~$40). LTV:CAC of ~1.3:1. Needs significant retention improvement to work.

**Score 4 example:** B2B SaaS at $499/month. CAC estimated at $2,000 via content marketing and sales. Average contract is 18 months (LTV ~$9,000). LTV:CAC of 4.5:1. Payback in 4 months.
