# Trend Analysis Reference

## Trend Categorization Framework

Trends operate at three levels. Effective market analysis identifies which level is
driving the opportunity.

### Mega Trends (10-30 year horizon)

Large-scale structural shifts that reshape entire economies.

| Trend | Direction | Market Impact |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Aging population | Accelerating in developed economies | Healthcare, elder care, financial planning |
| Urbanization | 68% urban by 2050 (UN) | Housing, mobility, infrastructure, delivery |
| Climate transition | Regulatory and market driven | Energy, transportation, agriculture, materials |
| Digital transformation | Ongoing across all industries | Cloud, AI, cybersecurity, automation |
| Decentralization | Early stage, sector-dependent | Finance, identity, governance, supply chain |

### Macro Trends (3-10 year horizon)

Industry-level shifts that create or destroy market categories.

Examples:
- Remote/hybrid work normalization (2020-present)
- Generative AI adoption (2023-present)
- Creator economy maturation
- Embedded finance / BaaS proliferation
- Zero-trust security architecture adoption

### Micro Trends (1-3 year horizon)

Product and behavior-level shifts that indicate near-term demand.

Examples:
- Specific product category growth spikes
- Platform migration patterns (e.g., social media shifts)
- Pricing model evolution (subscription fatigue, usage-based)
- Channel preference changes (short-form video, podcast growth)

## Identifying Relevant Trends

For a given market opportunity, map the trend stack:

```
1. Which mega trend creates structural demand?
2. Which macro trend shapes the industry response?
3. Which micro trends signal near-term timing?
```

A strong opportunity sits at the intersection of all three levels. An opportunity
driven only by a micro trend has limited longevity.

## Technology Adoption Lifecycle (Rogers Curve)

### Stages

| Stage | Market Penetration | Buyer Profile | Marketing Approach |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Innovators | 0-2.5% | Technology enthusiasts, risk-tolerant | Community, direct outreach |
| Early Adopters | 2.5-16% | Visionaries, strategic buyers | Case studies, ROI demonstration |
| Early Majority | 16-50% | Pragmatists, need proven solutions | References, integrations, support |
| Late Majority | 50-84% | Conservatives, buy on necessity | Price competition, bundling |
| Laggards | 84-100% | Skeptics, forced by circumstance | Compliance, mandates |

### The Chasm

The gap between Early Adopters and Early Majority is where most products fail. Crossing
the chasm requires:

- A complete product (not just technology)
- Referenceable customers in the target segment
- A repeatable sales process
- Ecosystem support (integrations, partners, support infrastructure)

### Determining Current Stage

Signals to assess where a market sits on the adoption curve:

| Signal | Innovators | Early Adopters | Early Majority | Late Majority |
|--------|-----------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Venture funding | Pre-seed/seed | Series A-B | Series C+/IPO | Consolidation |
| Media coverage | Tech blogs only | Business press | Mainstream press | Background noise |
| Competitive landscape | 2-5 startups | 10-20 competitors | Market leaders emerge | Oligopoly |
| Buyer behavior | Seeks novelty | Seeks advantage | Seeks reliability | Seeks lowest cost |
| Standards | None | Emerging | Established | Mandated |
| Revenue models | Experimental | Finding fit | Proven, scaling | Commoditized |

## Timing Assessment Criteria

### Market Timing Score

Evaluate five dimensions, each scored 1-5:

1. **Problem urgency** — Is the pain acute enough that buyers actively seek solutions now?
2. **Solution readiness** — Does the enabling technology work well enough for mainstream use?
3. **Ecosystem maturity** — Are distribution channels, integrations, and support
   infrastructure available?
4. **Competitive window** — Is there space to enter, or has the market already consolidated?
5. **Regulatory clarity** — Are the rules established enough to build a business on?

| Total Score | Timing Assessment |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 20-25 | Optimal — market is ready and window is open |
| 15-19 | Favorable — most conditions align, some gaps manageable |
| 10-14 | Early — opportunity exists but timing risk is significant |
| 5-9 | Premature — market conditions not yet supportive |

## Signal vs Noise Identification

### Strong Signals (Weight Heavily)

- Sustained revenue growth in adjacent companies (public filings)
- Increasing venture capital deal volume AND deal size in the category
- Government regulation or mandate creating forced adoption
- Large incumbents launching competitive products (validates the market)
- Measurable behavior change in target customers (not just stated intent)

### Weak Signals (Monitor, Don't Act On)

- Social media hype without corresponding revenue
- Single analyst report without corroborating data
- Conference buzz without product-market fit evidence
- Survey data showing "intent to purchase" (stated preference is unreliable)
- Celebrity/influencer endorsements without underlying demand data

### Noise (Discard)

- Viral moments with no structural driver
- Year-over-year comparisons against anomalous base periods (COVID distortions)
- Market size estimates without methodology disclosure
- Growth rates calculated from a near-zero base ("500% growth" from $10K to $50K)
- Press releases about partnerships with no announced revenue impact

## Leading Indicators by Industry

### SaaS / Enterprise Software
- Job postings mentioning the product category (leading by 6-12 months)
- Open source project activity (GitHub stars, contributors, forks)
- Developer community growth (Stack Overflow questions, Discord members)
- Conference talk submissions on the topic

### Consumer / E-commerce
- Google Trends search volume (leading by 3-6 months)
- Social media mention volume and sentiment
- Influencer content creation rate in the category
- Amazon search rank and review velocity for comparable products

### Healthcare / Biotech
- Clinical trial registrations (leading by 2-5 years)
- FDA guidance documents and draft regulations
- CMS reimbursement code additions
- Academic publication volume in the therapeutic area

### Fintech
- Regulatory sandbox admissions
- Banking-as-a-Service partnership announcements
- Open banking API adoption rates
- Central bank digital currency pilot programs

### Hardware / Manufacturing
- Patent filing volume (leading by 1-3 years)
- Component cost curves (semiconductor, battery, sensor pricing)
- Supply chain capacity investment (fab construction, factory expansion)
- Industry standard development activity

## Trend Lifespan Assessment

| Indicator | Short-lived (< 2 years) | Durable (5+ years) |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Driver | Single event, novelty | Structural shift, economics |
| Adoption pattern | Spike then decline | S-curve, sustained growth |
| Investment pattern | Hype-driven, speculative | Infrastructure-focused, strategic |
| Regulatory response | Reactive, uncertain | Proactive, framework-oriented |
| Supply side | Few suppliers, constrained | Growing ecosystem, competition |
