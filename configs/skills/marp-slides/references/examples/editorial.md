### Chapter One

# The Morning Begins Before You Wake

> A good morning is not an event but a conversation — between the night you had and the day you are about to face.

Most advice treats mornings as a performance: a sequence of habits to execute. The alternative is quieter. You arrange the night so that the morning has nothing to prove.

**Three small arrangements** that do more than any 5 a.m. alarm:

- Put the coffee maker where you'll see it first.
- Leave one book open on the counter — not the phone.
- Set tomorrow's clothes out like an invitation, not a uniform.

### Chapter Two

# On the Usefulness of Small Windows

Thirty minutes is not enough to change your life. It is enough to remember what it's like to have one.

A small window, chosen well:

1. **Fifteen minutes of reading** — not productive reading, the other kind.
2. **Ten minutes of walking** — no headphones, no podcast.
3. **Five minutes of nothing** — actually nothing.

The compounding is not in minutes. It is in the distance between the person who begins the window and the person who closes it.

# The Tools That Helped

| Tool | Why it stayed |
|---|---|
| **A paper notebook** | It never asks for an account. |
| **A kitchen timer** | It cannot check the weather. |
| **One mug, chipped** | It has a history that isn't mine. |
| **A garden, small** | It teaches patience by refusing to hurry. |

None of these scale. That is the point.
